About State of Palestine

What Is Palestine?,The word Palestine derives from the Greek word, Philistia, which dates to Ancient Greek writers' descriptions of the region in the 12th century B.C. Since the fall of the Ottoman Empire in World War I to 1918, Palestine typically referred to the geographic region located between the Mediterranean Sea and the Jordan River. Arab people who call this territory home have been known as Palestinians since the early 20th century. Much of this land is now considered present-day Israel. Today, Palestine theoretically includes the West Bank (a territory that sits between modern-day Israel and Jordan) and the Gaza Strip (which borders modern-day Israel and Egypt). However, control over this region is a complex and evolving situation. There is no international consensus concerning the borders, and many areas claimed by Palestinians have been occupied by Israelis for years. More than 135 United Nations member countries recognize Palestine as an independent state, but Israel and some other countries, including the United States, don’t make this distinction.

List of Time Period

Early Muslim Period

  1. - 637 (or 638) – Jerusalem falls to the armies of Rashidun caliph Umar Ibn el-Khatab. Jews are permitted to return to the city after 568 years of Roman and Byzantine rule.
  2. - 661 – The Umayyad family takes control of the caliphate and moves its capital to Damascus, following the assassination of the Caliph Ali ibn Abi Talib.
  3. - 747–750 – Civil war resulting in the overthrow of the Umayyads, the Abbasid family seize control of the caliphate.
  4. - 969/70 – The Fatimids, a self-proclaimed Shia caliphate, defeat the Ikhshidids and appoint a Jewish governor.

Crusader/Ayyubid period

  1. - 1096–1099 – First Crusade and the establishment of the Catholic Kingdom of Jerusalem in Outremer.
  2. - 1099 June 7 – The crusaders reach Jerusalem and besieges the city.
  3. - 1099 August 12 – The Crusaders defeat the Fatimids at the Battle of Ascalon.
  4. - 1100 December 25 – The Kingdom of Jerusalem is established.
  5. - 1187 May 1 – Battle of Cresson: Saladin defeats the crusaders.
  6. - 1187 October 2 – Saladin captures Jerusalem from Crusaders.
  7. - 1189–1192 – Third Crusade led by the armies of Richard the Lionhearted
  8. - 1191 September 7 – Richard I of England defeats Saladin at the Battle of Arsuf forcing him to retreat with heavy losses.
  9. - 1192 September 2 – Richard and Saladin signs the Treaty of Jaffa, a peace-treaty to run for three years.
  10. - 1192 October 9 – Richard leaves Palestine
  11. - 1193 March 3/4 – Saladin dies in Damascus. Conflicts between his sons, brothers and nephews cause the disintegration of his empire.
  12. - 1260 – Battle of Ain Jalut (Jezreel Valley) between the Egyptian Mamluks and the Mongols.
  13. - 1265 – The Mamluk Bahri dynasty of Egypt captures several cities and towns from Crusader states in the Middle East, including the cities of Haifa, Arsuf, and Caesarea Maritima.
  14. - 1291 May 18 – Fall of Acre: Al-Ashraf Khalil of Egypt captures Acre, thus exterminating the Crusader Kingdom of Jerusalem ( the final Catholic landholding remaining from the Crusades), and ending the Ninth Crusade.
